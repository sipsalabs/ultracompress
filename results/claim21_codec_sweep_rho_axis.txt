KEY OBSERVATIONS
----------------
- brotli-11 and lzma-6 dominate at every rho (top-2 savings).
- zstd-22 very close to lzma-6 but with much faster decode.
- lz4-hc saves ~0% - NEGATIVE CONTROL. The payload does NOT
  compress under fast dictionary coders, confirming the gains
  above are true entropy-coder savings on structured content,
  not accidental low-hanging fruit.
- bz2-9 trails the entropy coders by ~5 percentage points at
  every rho - BWT is a poor fit for the fp8 float-byte tail,
  evidence that savings are codec-family-specific.
- Savings are MONOTONE-INCREASING with rho for every codec,
  showing Claim-21 scales cleanly as the restored-row budget
  grows (larger payload -> richer context for the coder).
