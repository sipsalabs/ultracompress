Claim-21 idx_delta per-byte-position diagnostic
==================================================================================
Reshape idx_delta as (N, 4) (little-endian int32 lanes) and
compute Shannon H of each byte position independently.

If the 4 byte lanes were mutually independent, any coder's
achievable rate is bounded below by sum(H_pos_i) / 4 bits/byte.
The gap between that bound and the as-emitted full-stream H
measures cross-lane dependence (= joint savings beyond per-lane).

INTERPRETATION
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- MSB lanes (bytes 2, 3) have near-100%% zeros: deltas are small
  positive integers (sorted-row indices advance slowly; since
  restored rows are sparse, gaps between adjacent indices are
  a few hundred at most). This is why byte permutation kills
  savings sharply (wave 17: lzma-6 idx_delta -14.0pp): the
  position-of-zero-byte information is what lzma exploits.
- LSB lane (byte 0) carries most of the entropy (H near 6-7 bpB)
  because the low byte of a small integer is essentially random.
- The gap between sum-per-byte savings floor and the full-stream
  order-0 floor measures cross-lane dependence: if Hsum/4 (in bpB)
  equals full_H, byte lanes are marginally independent; any
  additional savings from positional structure come from here.
