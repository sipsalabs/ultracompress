Claim-21 order-0 byte-histogram diagnostic
==================================================================================
For each stream, measure how far the byte distribution deviates
from uniform. These deviations are the fundamental source of
order-0 compressibility (wave 17 established that fp8 savings
are ~89% order-0). Here we quantify 'how compressible must it
be in principle', as an information-theoretic lower bound on
bits/byte no coder can beat without exploiting context.

Columns:
  H (bits/B)  = Shannon entropy of byte histogram (uniform = 8.0000)
  floor %     = order-0 savings floor = 100 * (8 - H) / 8
                (= lower bound on savings achievable by any coder)
  TV          = total-variation distance from uniform (0..1)
  chi2/n      = chi-square / n_bytes (deviation, scale-free)
  max/mean    = most-frequent-byte count / expected count at uniform

INTERPRETATION
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- fp8 H < 8 bpB directly: its byte histogram is measurably
  sub-uniform. The order-0 savings floor is the minimum
  compression ratio any coder (even one that ignores context)
  could achieve. This is the fundamental cause of the
  ~16 pp of fp8 savings that survive byte permutation (wave 17).
- Hadamard rotation + FP8 quantization produces a byte
  distribution that is provably, quantitatively non-uniform;
  the sub-uniformity is a property of the encoding, not of
  any particular model.
