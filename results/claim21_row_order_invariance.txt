INTERPRETATION
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- fp8:   < 0.02% variation across orderings (order-invariant) -- per-row
         local byte distribution is the dominant compressibility signal.
- scale: < 0.5% variation (order-invariant).
- idx:   shuffled order inflates idx bytes by +34%..+86% vs sorted;
         reversed matches sorted to within 2-5%. Delta-coding of sorted
         indices generates small positive deltas that compress well;
         shuffling produces near-uniform random deltas. Reversed produces
         small NEGATIVE deltas with identical |delta| distribution, which
         encodes just as well.
- TOTAL impact on Claim 21 savings: fp8 is ~98-99% of total compressed
         bytes, so total savings are order-invariant to < 0.05%. The idx
         sensitivity, while internally large, is on a stream that is 3-4
         orders of magnitude smaller than fp8.

CONCLUSION: Claim 21 fp8/scale savings come from per-row intrinsic byte-
distribution structure, NOT from clever row ordering. The idx-stream
savings ARE order-dependent, but the natural sort-by-index emission order
is free (zero computational cost) and is what current Claim 21 uses.
