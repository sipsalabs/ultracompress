Claim-21 wave 26: scale stream fp16 pair decomposition
==========================================================================================

Wave 21 showed idx_delta's int32 layout is 2/4 bytes
structurally zero. This wave does the equivalent measurement
for the fp16 scale stream: per-byte H, joint H over the
65536-bin pair distribution, and mutual information between
the two bytes of each fp16. Also reports the Shannon H of the
sign (1-bit), exponent (5-bit), and mantissa-hi/lo (2+8-bit)
fields, which is an information-theoretic tight lower bound
on any independent-field coder.

  model            byte0 H   byte1 H       sum     joint    I(B0;B1)   field sum  field bpB
  olmo2_1b          7.8108    3.1458   10.9566   10.4513      0.5053     11.1226     5.5613
  qwen3_1.7b        5.1266    3.0687    8.1953    7.9278      0.2675      8.4055     4.2027
  smollm2_1.7b      5.1816    3.2436    8.4252    8.1582      0.2670      8.5443     4.2722
  tinyllama         5.1592    3.6460    8.8052    8.4745      0.3306      8.8477     4.4239

  Cohort (unweighted mean over 4 models):
  mean              5.8196    3.2760    9.0956    8.7530      0.3426      9.2300     4.6150

For context: wave 19 reported scale stream Shannon H (byte-level
mixed across both byte positions) cohort-aggregate ~5.39 bpB. The
per-byte-position H here is the actually-realisable floor for any
positionally-aware coder; the joint H is the tightest achievable
lower bound for any coder that sees both bytes of each fp16 as one
unit; the field-decomposed H is the lower bound for any coder that
splits fp16 into its IEEE 754 sign/exp/mantissa fields.
