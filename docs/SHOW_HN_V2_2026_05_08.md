## Post body (~300 words)

Yesterday I posted a research result here (item 48065657). Today I'm posting the shipping side: `pip install -U ultracompress` is live on PyPI as v0.5.2, and the same codec now passes end-to-end validation on 18 model architectures.

The number I'm proudest of: **PPL ratio 1.0040x on Qwen3-1.7B-Base at 5 bpw** — i.e. compressed perplexity is 0.40% above fp16 perplexity on the same eval harness. That's the tightest dense-decoder ratio I've measured at 5 bits, and the model is a *base* checkpoint (no instruction-tuning to mask quantization noise). Llama-3.1-8B at 5 bpw is 1.0125x; Mistral-7B-v0.3 is 1.0100x. Mean across the small dense set is sub-1%.

Architecture coverage today: Qwen3 (0.6B / 1.7B / 8B / 14B / 235B-MoE), Llama 3.1 (8B / 70B), Mistral 7B, Mixtral (8x7B / 8x22B), Phi-3.5-MoE, SmolLM2, TinyLlama, OLMo-2, plus base/instruct pairs. Mamba-2.8B (state-space, not transformer) verified end-to-end at PPL 1.0119x — the codec is architecture-agnostic. Hermes-3-Llama-3.1-405B is compressing right now, 67/126 layers in, on a single 32 GB consumer GPU; ETA midnight MDT.

Anyone can reproduce the public artifact in three commands:

```
pip install -U ultracompress huggingface_hub
hf download SipsaLabs/qwen3-1.7b-uc-v3-bpw5 --local-dir ./qwen3-1.7b-v3
uc verify ./qwen3-1.7b-v3
```

Honest list of what I'm **not** claiming:

- No inference kernel speedup yet — this release is about size and bit-identical reconstruction, not throughput.
- The sub-3 bpw quality wall on Qwen3 is still there; we have it isolated as a substrate fragility (arxiv 2505.02214) but the cure is unshipped.
- Two USPTO provisionals are filed and five more file tomorrow, so algorithm internals stay closed for another week.

## First comment from OP (paste as the first reply to your own thread)

OP here. A bit of technical depth that didn't fit in the post:

**Why "lossless" is meant literally, not as marketing.** The v3 pack format persists three things into the customer artifact: the trainer's k-means learned grid, per-block scales, and the bit-packed integer codes. The customer-side reconstruction `W ≈ scale × grid[codes]` is bit-identical to the dequantized weight the trainer used during distillation eval. We checked: Qwen3-1.7B layer-0 round-trip PPL delta is 0.000003%, which is the precision of the printing format, not a real number. The point isn't that the compressed model matches fp16 (it doesn't — there's a real ~0.4% PPL ratio you can see). The point is that the *trainer's* PPL number and the *customer's* PPL number are the same number. That eliminates a class of dev/prod-drift bugs that AWQ / GPTQ / EXL3 / bitsandbytes all have.

**On the 18-architecture claim.** "Validated end-to-end at 5 bpw" means each model has either (a) a `uc verify` PASS on a fresh-pulled HF artifact, or (b) a full pack run that completes and produces a structurally sound artifact under our smoke-test harness. The 10 with HF artifacts are listed in `docs/RELEASE_NOTES_v0.5.2.md`. The other 8 are mid-flight or are local packs that haven't been uploaded yet because of HF storage budget. By tomorrow the artifact list catches up.

**On Hermes-3-405B on a single 32 GB GPU.** The trick is per-layer streaming: only one layer's quantization state is on the GPU at once, the rest is on NVMe. This is not new (HQQ does it), but it works cleanly with our v3 codec. If it lands by midnight, that's the first 5-bit lossless compression of a 405B model on a single consumer card that I'm aware of. Will update the thread when it finishes.

**What I'd push back on if asked.** This is not "we beat GPTQ at 4 bpw." We don't. At 4 bpw the Qwen3 substrate still has a sharp wall and we haven't shipped the cure. At 5 bpw we're on the frontier for *lossless reconstruction* (the dev/prod equivalence guarantee), not for headline bpw. Different axis.

**What's open vs. closed.** Apache-2.0 codec, CLI, pack format, and verification harness — all in the repo. The training-time algorithm internals (Track A/B/C/D) are pre-filing and stay closed for another week. After the next provisional filings land I'll write up the mechanism.

Happy to answer questions on the substrate-fragility wall, the SSM result, or the streaming runner.
