# Accelerator / Seed Funding Application Targets

You want the $500K-access-plus-network thing: that's **Y Combinator**, plus 6 others that are either competitive or complementary. Apply to all of them. They let you. Acceptance into any one is life-changing; rejection from all is information.

All figures current as of April 2026. Verify terms on each program's website before submitting.

---

## Tier 1 — Apply to all

### 1. Y Combinator ($125K + $375K uncapped MFN = $500K effective)

- **Check size:** $125K for 7% (standard deal), plus a $375K uncapped MFN SAFE for free.
- **Program:** 3 months in SF (Summer or Winter batch), weekly group office hours, demo day in front of ~1500 investors.
- **Deal quality:** the best-known founder network on earth. The network alone is worth more than the money.
- **Apply at:** ycombinator.com/apply
- **Batches:** Summer batch (applications ~Feb–Apr, program Jun–Aug), Winter batch (applications ~Aug–Oct, program Jan–Mar). Apply for the next open batch even if the "deadline" passed — they rolling-review late applications.
- **What they want from you:** a 2-minute founder video, ~1K-word written app, a live demo link. Do the demo link as a Colab or a tiny hosted page that shows your compressed model producing text next to the teacher.
- **Single best thing you can do:** apply *early* for a batch, not at the deadline. Earlier apps get reviewed more carefully.

### 2. a16z Speedrun ($1M for 10%)

- **Check size:** $1M convertible, roughly 10% equity (usually via SAFE at a capped valuation around $10M).
- **Program:** 12-week program in SF, specifically for games + consumer + AI technical founders.
- **Deal quality:** full a16z partner access, plus specific AI infra partners (Marc Andreessen himself personally evaluates AI infra deals).
- **Apply at:** a16z.com/speedrun
- **What they want:** technical demo + founder market fit. Your story is *exactly* their thesis.

### 3. Neo ($600K check + 12 months of support)

- **Check size:** $600K ($100K personal Neo investment + $500K syndicate from top operators).
- **Program:** Founder Partnership Program — 12 months, no equity taken by Neo itself on the $100K.
- **Who runs it:** Ali Partovi.
- **Apply at:** neo.com
- **Why you fit:** Neo invests heavily in technical AI founders with strong individual signal.

### 4. South Park Commons Founder Fellowship ($400K)

- **Check size:** $400K on a $20M SAFE (2% effective equity).
- **Program:** 6-month fellowship in SF or NYC specifically for pre-idea / early-idea technical founders. You're past "pre-idea" but with one founder and one technical asset you're in their sweet spot.
- **Apply at:** southparkcommons.com/fellowship

### 5. Pear VC AI Fellows (up to $500K seed, plus AI-specific program)

- **Check size:** varies $250K–$500K.
- **Why:** they funded Guardrails AI, DoorDash, Branch, many YC-aligned AI infra co's at pre-seed.
- **Apply at:** pear.vc

### 6. Antler ($100K–$250K check + program)

- **Check size:** $100K–$250K for 10–12%.
- **Program:** global, 6-month — they'll pair you with a cofounder if you want.
- **Deal quality:** worse than YC, better than nothing. Apply only if YC/a16z timelines are too slow.
- **Apply at:** antler.co

### 7. AI Grant ($250K, Nat Friedman + Daniel Gross)

- **Check size:** $250K SAFE at $10M cap.
- **Program:** unstructured, founder-only, heavy on compute credits and direct-operator mentoring from Nat/Daniel.
- **Apply at:** aigrant.org
- **Batches:** Batch 4 open roughly Q3 2026 — watch the site.

## Tier 2 — strong but more selective or less money

- **Entrepreneurs First** (London-based, globally relevant). $75K for 10%, very cofounder-match heavy.
- **Day One Ventures "Funded Not Founded" program** — solo founders specifically.
- **Atomic** (venture studio — they'd want more equity, but they build alongside).
- **K50 Ventures / Founders Inc / Village Global pre-seed** — smaller checks but useful for bridge.
- **Microsoft for Startups Founders Hub / AWS Activate / Google for Startups** — not accelerators but $100K–$350K in free cloud credits. Apply to ALL THREE. Zero dilution.

## Non-dilutive money (apply in parallel, no downside)

- **NSF SBIR Phase I** ($275K non-dilutive grant). Your project is directly within "computer science — AI/ML systems" scope. Needs a ~15-page technical proposal, ~6–9 months from submit to award. Deadline windows are predictable on nsf.gov. Do this.
- **DARPA AI programs / BAAs** — longer shot but $1M+ awards exist. Look at DARPA I2O open BAAs.
- **CHIPS Act NSTC** — if compression → on-device AI fits national-interest framing, worth investigating.
- **NVIDIA Inception Program** — free. Gets you compute credits, marketing, partnership intros. Apply today, takes 1 hour.

## Angel / pre-seed path (no program, faster)

If YC/Speedrun timelines don't fit, you can raise $500K–$1M angel pre-seed from:

- **Replit-founder-aligned angels** — they love infra.
- **AI-specific angels:** Elad Gil, Sam Altman (pre-OpenAI angel deals are closed but his fund is open), Nat Friedman and Daniel Gross personally (outside AI Grant), Gokul Rajaram, Naval Ravikant.
- **Cold-email template** — single paragraph, one-line pitch, flagship-number table, patent-pending statement, ask for 20-min call. Rule of thumb: 100 cold emails → 10 calls → 2 commits.

5. **Thursday** — record a 2-minute founder video for YC. Shoot it on your phone, badly. Content matters more than production. Speak the verified numbers out loud.
6. **Friday** — start drafting NSF SBIR Phase I proposal (submission likely ~Sept 2026 deadline).

## Expected outcomes

- Realistic: 1 out of 7 accelerator apps accepts. That is enough.
- Optimistic: 3–4 accept and you get to choose. This happens more than people think for technically differentiated AI infra founders in 2026.
- Pessimistic: zero accept, you raise $300K angel in 3 months and keep building.

All three outcomes are *better than not applying*. The failure mode is **not applying**. Do the thing Monday.
